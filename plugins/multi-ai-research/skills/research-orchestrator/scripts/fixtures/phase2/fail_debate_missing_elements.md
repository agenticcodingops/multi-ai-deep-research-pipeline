## Position: FOR

The image-pipeline generator remains the right choice for a hobby photography blog. The opposing case concentrates on raw rebuild speed, but the daily workflow of a photographer is dominated by image handling and theme quality, and on both of those axes the incumbent is clearly ahead. Speed differences at the scale of a few hundred photographs stay inside the noise floor of any realistic measurement, so the deciding factors are the ones the incumbent already wins. This restates the stance taken in the opening round without qualification.

## Evidence and reasoning

The build-time image pipeline is documented behaviour rather than marketing: https://example.org/docs/image-pipeline describes the derivative generation that keeps gallery pages light without manual export presets, and it is the single feature that removes the most recurring manual work from a photography workflow. Theme upkeep is independently verifiable through the registry listing at https://example.org/docs/gallery-themes, where the three most installed portfolio themes all shipped releases within the past year and their issue queues receive maintainer replies within days. Free hosting tiers absorb an image-heavy hobby blog without bandwidth pressure, with per-site storage as the only practical ceiling, so operating cost is a wash between the candidates. Taken together, the tooling evidence outweighs a speed advantage that only materialises at library sizes this project will not reach, and nothing in the record shows the incumbent failing at the scale that matters here.
